North West Branch

A beautiful place to hike, climb, and see local art

Posted by Emily Shue on November 27, 2018

Northwest Branch (NWB) is the perfect place to try outdoor bouldering for the first time or put your skills to the test on harder classics (see The Business, V6). Not only is the location accessible with ample parking, there are plenty of problems to try.

Convience


Parking

There is a large parking area with the trailhead clearly marked. Additionally, there are trash bins right at the trailhead if needed. No designated restrooms, but there are stores a two minute walk away from the parking lot.

Approach

Most of the climbs are along the trail but a couple require stream crossings. As you travel to climbs further down the trail, some climbs, such as at the Long Wall Area require hiking up hills. The general pace is hike a bit down the trail, try a couple climbs, and continue walking down the trail to the next prominent boulder. Some of the most convenient climbs I tried include Don’t Fall Right (V0)/Go Left (V0) and Snowflake (V2). Both are near the trailhead and make good warm-ups.

Quality of Climbs

I’ve visited NWB twice and enjoyed the variety of climbs available. There is a good range of slab and vert climbs. There are even a couple dynos. On the downside, trash and graffiti can be an issue in some areas. Overall, I think there is lots more to be explored in the area. Hopefully there will be an updated guide coming soon.

Some cool climbs I tried

  • The Edge of Chaos (V3)-- a cool jump onto a neat boulder
  • The Traverse (V5)
  • Long Wall Area-- lots of fun climbs here
